Works Cited

Beauchamp, Travis. (2013). The Smash Brothers. United States. Beauchamp’s documentary focuses on the early to mid-years of competitive Super Smash Bros. Melee. There is a strong focus on the lives of the players and the players that propelled the game to the prestigious position it is in today.

Budding, E. (2018, December 3). The Book of Melee: The beginnings of Smash history [Blog post]. Budding describes the grassroots beginning of the competitive Super Smash Bros. Melee scene. Effectively shares information about the key players and tournaments that allowed Melee to become what it is today. Touches on Ken and Azen and references the Smash Brothers documentary.

Dilly-Jo. (2018, December 6). Movement Options in Melee [Blog post]. Retrieved from http://team-dignitas.net/articles/blogs/smash/13198/movement-options-in-melee-part-1-the-basics Dilly-Jo explains some basic movement mechanics in Super Smash Bros. Melee. He also goes on to explain so more in depth and technical movement options.

GameCube Fighting Game of the Year: Super Smash Bros. Melee. (2001). Retrieved September 23, 2019, from GameSpy.com website: http://web.archive.org/web/20071220220600/http://archive.gamespy.com/goty2001/gc/gc_fighting_winner.shtml A report on Fighting Game of the Year in 2001. It discusses the game and its improvements from its previous iteration.

Goslin, A. (2018, September 18). Super Smash Bros. pro player Armada retires from solo competition [Blog post]. Retrieved from https://www.polygon.com/2018/9/18/17876286/super-smash-bros-armada-retires-singles-tournaments Goslin discuess Armada's retirement from competitive Super Smash Bros. Melee and the implications surrounding the move. Armada is considered the best player to ever pick up the controller and will cause a massive change by leaving the scene.

Leendertsen, J. (2017, April 16). How Super Smash Bros. Melee Has Stood The Test Of Time. Game Informer. Retrieved from https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2017/04/16/how-super-smash-bros-melee-has-stood-the-test-of-time.aspx Leendertsen focuses on what makes Super Smash Bros. Melee such a great game and the competitive scene that surrounds the game. Focuses on key players late in the games lifespan, name the Five Gods, who carried Melee through the 2010's.

Starkley, D. (2016, July 9). How the hell is Super Smash Bros. Melee still this popular? Retrieved September 23, 2019, from https://www.geek.com/tech/how-the-hell-is-smash-bros-melee-still-this-popular-1662268/ Starkley examines the spectacle of longevity the Super Smash Bros. Melee presents. Explains why the game has lasted as long as it has and shines a light on the current state of the competitive scene.

Why 'Super Smash Bros. Melee' Is the Game to Watch at Evo 2016 [Blog post]. (2016, July 7). Retrieved from https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/ppakvg/why-super-smash-bros-melee-is-the-game-to-watch-at-evo-2016 Gee explains the implications surrounding Super Smash Bros. Melee's spot at Evo 2016. Examines the viewership and participation of the tournament and how it was the biggest Melee tournament of all time.

Wolf, J. (2019, August 15). Melee community navigates a world without Evo [Blog post]. Retrieved from ttps://www.espn.com/esports/story/_/id/27396377/melee-community-navigates-world-evo Wolf discusses the implications of Super Smash Bros. Melee no longer being featured at Evo starting in 2019. Examines how the community will survive without the biggest fighting game tournament series.
