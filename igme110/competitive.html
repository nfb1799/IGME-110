Competitive Melee

Super Smash Bros. Melee is one of the most popular esports in 2019, 18 years after the game's release. Never fully receiving the support of Nintendo, the explosion of the competitive scene was a massive grassroots effort.

The first competitive Melee tournament was held in a player's basement on April 6, 2002. It was advertised on an online forum and had a turnout of about 20 players. This tournament sparked a massive uprising of small tournament across the country.

During the early years of the competitive scene, a brand called Major League Gaming started a Super Smash Bros. Melee circuit making it possible for players to call themselves "professional" and to make a living playing this video game.

Players like Ken and Azen constantly pushed each other and the game to their limits, showing spectators what was truly possible in this game. They were the pioners of Super Smash Bros. Melee and inspired thousands upon thousands of players to play the very best game they could.
